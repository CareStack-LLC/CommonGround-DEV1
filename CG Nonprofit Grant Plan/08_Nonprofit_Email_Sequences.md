# Nonprofit Email Outreach Sequences

## Overview
This document contains proven email templates for reaching out to nonprofit organizations serving DV survivors and high-conflict families. Each sequence emphasizes partnership, impact, and zero cost to the organization.

---

## Email Sequence 1: Cold Outreach (Primary)

### Email 1A: The Impact Extension Angle

**Subject Line Options:**
1. "Free technology for your DV survivors"
2. "Extend your program's impact—no cost"
3. "Partnership opportunity: [Nonprofit Name] + CommonGround"

**Email Body:**

```
Hi [Contact First Name],

I'm reaching out because CommonGround is partnering with organizations like [Nonprofit Name] to provide free co-parenting tools for DV survivors and families in crisis.

The challenge we keep hearing: Clients complete your program stronger and safer, but the co-parenting conflict continues after they leave. You lose touch and can't provide ongoing support.

What we're offering:
- Free AI-mediated co-parenting platform for all your clients (Pro tier, $40/month value)
- Automated outcomes tracking for your grant reports
- Co-branded landing page showing your organization's support
- Zero cost to you or your clients

We're currently partnering with [X] organizations in SoCal serving [Y] families.

Would you be open to a 15-20 minute call to explore if this could support [Nonprofit Name]'s mission?

[Calendar Link]

Best,
[Your Name]
[Title]
CommonGround
[Phone]
[Email]

P.S. - We're a social enterprise—we make money from those who can afford to pay and subsidize access for those who can't.
```

**Why This Works:**
- Leads with "free" (removes financial barrier)
- Names their pain point (post-program support gap)
- Clear value proposition
- Social proof (other organizations)
- P.S. addresses "what's the catch?"

## Follow-Up Sequence

### Follow-Up 1 (5 Days After Initial Email - If No Response)

**Subject:** "Re: [Original Subject]"

**Email Body:**

```
Hi [Contact First Name],

Following up on my email from last week about providing free co-parenting tools for your clients at [Nonprofit Name].

I know you're incredibly busy serving your community, so I'll keep this brief.

We're finalizing partnerships with [Number] organizations in [Area] for our pilot program and wanted to make sure [Nonprofit Name] had a chance to participate.

Here's a 3-minute overview: [Link to Video or One-Pager]

Still interested in exploring this? Just grab 20 minutes here: [Calendar Link]

If the timing isn't right, totally understand—just let me know.

Best,
[Your Name]

P.S. - Happy to connect you with [Director Name] at [Similar Nonprofit] who's already using the platform. They can share their experience directly.
```

**Why This Works:**
- Acknowledges they're busy
- Creates gentle urgency (finalizing partnerships)
- Provides easy way to learn more (video)
- Offers peer reference (reduces risk)
- Permission to decline

---

### Follow-Up 2 (7 Days After Follow-Up 1 - If Still No Response)

**Subject:** "Not the right time?"

**Email Body:**

```
Hi [Contact First Name],

I haven't heard back, so I'm guessing either:

A) This isn't relevant for [Nonprofit Name] right now, or  
B) It fell off your radar amid everything else you're managing

If it's (A), no worries—I'll stop emailing. Just let me know.

If it's (B) and you'd still like to explore this, here's all the info in one place:

**What:** Free AI-powered co-parenting tools for your clients + automated outcomes tracking
**Cost:** $0 to you and your clients  
**Commitment:** Distribute grant codes, 15-min client orientation
**Results:** Extended program impact, data for grant reports, clients get ongoing support

Want to discuss? [Calendar Link]

Either way, huge respect for the work you're doing at [Nonprofit Name].

Best,
[Your Name]
CommonGround
```

**Why This Works:**
- Gives them an out (reduces pressure)
- Summarizes everything in bullets (easy to digest)
- Shows respect for their work
- Friendly, non-pushy tone

---

### Follow-Up 3 (30 Days Later - "Break-Up" Email)

**Subject:** "Final email"

**Email Body:**

```
Hi [Contact First Name],

This is my last email—promise!

I'm closing out outreach for [Area] nonprofits and wanted to see if there's anything I could have done differently to make this more relevant for [Nonprofit Name].

Even if you're not interested, any feedback would be genuinely helpful:
- Was the timing just not right?
- Does this not fit your current programs?
- Is there something else that would be more useful?

(I'm trying to get better at understanding nonprofit needs, so I really do appreciate honest feedback.)

Thanks for all you do for the community.

Best,
[Your Name]

P.S. - If you know another organization serving DV survivors or high-conflict families who might benefit from this, I'd be grateful for an intro.
```

**Why This Works:**
- Respectful exit
- Asks for learning (engages them differently)
- Humble tone
- Often gets responses when nothing else worked
- P.S. opens referral opportunity

### Tracking Metrics

For each email, track:
- **Sent:** Number of emails sent
- **Open Rate:** % who opened
- **Reply Rate:** % who replied
- **Positive Reply Rate:** % who replied with interest
- **Meeting Booked:** % who scheduled call
- **Meeting Held:** % who showed up
- **Partnership Signed:** % who became partners

**Target Benchmarks:**
- Open Rate: 35-50% (nonprofits often more responsive)
- Reply Rate: 8-15%
- Positive Reply Rate: 5-10%
- Meeting Booked: 3-7%

## Email Sequence Timing

### Optimal Send Times (Test in Your Market)

**Best Days:**
- Tuesday - Thursday (avoid Monday chaos and Friday winding down)
- Avoid end of month (grant deadlines, reporting)

**Best Times:**
- 9-11 AM (after morning email triage)

- Day 0: Initial email

## Email Deliverability Best Practices

Same principles as attorney outreach:

1. **Warm up domain** - Start small, scale gradually
2. **Authenticate** - SPF, DKIM, DMARC records
3. **Avoid spam triggers** - No ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation
4. **Clean lists** - Remove bounces, honor unsubscribes
5. **Monitor metrics** - Watch open rates for deliverability issues

**Nonprofit-Specific Considerations:**
- Many nonprofits use shared email domains (@gmail.com, @yahoo.com)
- These can be more likely to hit spam filters
- Consider providing multiple contact options (phone, LinkedIn)

## Red Flags to Watch For

Some nonprofits won't be good partners. Watch for:

❌ **No clear programs** - Vague mission, unclear who they serve
❌ **Tiny budget** (<$50K annual) - May lack capacity to distribute
❌ **No staff** - All volunteer orgs struggle with consistency
❌ **Irrelevant population** - Serve elderly, not families with children
❌ **Financially unstable** - Recent closure announcements, funding crises
❌ **Poor reputation** - Bad reviews, scandal, legal issues

**When in doubt:** Ask in discovery call, "How many families did you serve last year?" and "How many staff work directly with clients?"
