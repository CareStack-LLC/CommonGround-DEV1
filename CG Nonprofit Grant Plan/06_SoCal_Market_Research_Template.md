## Section 6: Pilot Program Design

### Target Pilot Size
- **Attorneys:** 5 firms (3 solo, 2 small firms)
- **Nonprofits:** 3 organizations (2 DV-focused, 1 legal aid)
- **Families:** 200 total (60 via nonprofit grants, 140 via attorney referrals)
- **Timeline:** 90 days to measure outcomes

### Success Metrics for Pilot

**For Attorneys:**
- Avg client communication time reduced by 50%+
- Case prep time reduced by 30%+
- Attorney satisfaction score: 4/5 or higher
- Would continue using after pilot: 80%+
- Referred another attorney: 60%+

**For Nonprofits:**
- Grant code activation rate: 60%+
- Client retention at 90 days: 50%+
- Conflict reduction: 25%+
- Client satisfaction (NPS): 50+
- Would continue partnership: 100%

**For Families:**
- ARIA acceptance rate: 60%+
- Weekly active usage: 70%+
- Self-reported conflict reduction: 50%+
- Legal fee savings: $2,000+ over 90 days
- NPS score: 40+

### Data to Collect During Pilot

- Daily: New signups, activation rates, usage metrics
- Weekly: ARIA intervention rates, message volume, feature adoption
- Monthly: User surveys, attorney interviews, nonprofit check-ins
- 90 Days: Full outcomes report, case studies, testimonials

### Market Size Estimation

**SoCal Total Addressable Market (TAM):**
- Population: ~24 million
- Divorce rate: ~3.2 per 1,000 = ~77,000 divorces/year
- Children involved in ~60% = ~46,000 custody cases/year
- High/moderate conflict: ~70% = ~32,000 high-conflict cases/year

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):**
- Families willing to pay for co-parenting tools: ~30% = ~9,600
- Avg LTV: $1,350 over 3 years
- SAM Revenue Potential: $13M/year at full penetration

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) - Year 1:**
- Market penetration: 2% of SAM = ~200 families
- Avg revenue per family: $480/year
- SOM Revenue: $96,000 from consumers

**Plus professional revenue:**
- 20 attorneys × $299/mo × 12 = $71,760
- Total Year 1 SOM: ~$168,000

*(This is conservative—project suggests $700K+ with scale)*
