# Attorney Discovery Call Script & Tracker

## Purpose
This script helps you qualify family law attorneys as potential CommonGround partners while gathering market intelligence. Use this for ALL initial attorney conversations.

---

## Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes)

### Research the Attorney
- [ ] LinkedIn profile reviewed
- [ ] Firm website reviewed (practice areas, team size, location)
- [ ] Google reviews checked (reputation, client sentiment)
- [ ] Any published articles or media presence noted
- [ ] Approximate firm size and revenue estimated

### Information to Gather
- Firm name and location
- Attorney name and title
- Years practicing family law
- Approximate caseload
- Current tools/systems used

## Call Structure (15 minutes total)

### Opening (1 minute)

**Your Script:**
"Hi [Attorney Name], this is [Your Name] from CommonGround. Thanks for taking my call. I know your time is valuable, so I'll be brief. We're building an AI-powered co-parenting platform specifically for high-conflict custody cases, and I'm talking to family law attorneys in [City] to understand what would actually be helpful. Is now still a good time for a quick 15-minute conversation?"

**If they say no:**
"No problem! When would work better for you? [Schedule follow-up]"

**If they say yes:**
"Perfect. Before I tell you anything about what we're building, I'd love to hear about your practice. Can I ask you a few questions?"

## Discovery Questions (10 minutes)

### Section 1: Practice Overview (2 minutes)

**Q1: "What percentage of your caseload is family law?"**

*What you're listening for:* 
- 50%+ = Primary target
- 30-50% = Secondary target
- <30% = Probably not a fit

*Follow-up based on answer:*
- If 50%+: "Great, so family law is your core focus"
- If <50%: "Got it. What other areas do you practice?"

**Q2: "How many active family law cases do you typically have at any given time?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Solo practitioner: 10-30 cases = Normal
- Small firm: 40-100 cases = Good fit
- Large firm: 100+ = Enterprise opportunity

*Record the number:* _______

**Q3: "What percentage of those involve moderate to high conflict?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- 50%+ = They deal with our core use case regularly
- <30% = May not see enough value

*Record the percentage:* _______

---

### Section 2: Pain Point Discovery (3 minutes)

**Q4: "Walk me through a typical week with your high-conflict cases. What takes up most of your time?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Client communication management (emails, texts, calls)
- Document organization and evidence compilation
- Scheduling coordination nightmares
- Financial dispute resolution
- Court prep and filing

*Take detailed notes:* ___________________________________

**Q5: "How do your clients currently communicate with their co-parent about the kids?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Text messages (chaotic, hard to track)
- Email (slightly better but still messy)
- OurFamilyWizard/other platform (current competitor user)
- Phone calls (worst for documentation)
- "Through me" (attorney as middleman = expensive)

*Record their answer:* _______

**Q6: "When clients come to you with 'he said, she said' disputes, how do you typically resolve those?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Pain around lack of documentation
- Time spent piecing together evidence
- Challenges in court when neither parent has proof
- Frustration with screenshots of texts

*Record their process:* _______

**Q7: "On average, how much time per week do you spend on case management vs. actual legal strategy or court prep?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- If case management > 50% = Strong pain point
- If mostly legal strategy = Either very organized or not dealing with high-conflict

*Record time split:* Case Management: ______ hrs | Legal Strategy: ______ hrs

---

### Section 3: Current Solutions (2 minutes)

**Q8: "Do you currently recommend any co-parenting apps or tools to your clients?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- OurFamilyWizard = Direct competitor
- Talking Parents = Direct competitor
- Nothing = Great opportunity
- Calendar apps/Venmo = No structured solution

*Record what they use:* _______

**If they use a competitor, ask:**
"What do you like about [Tool]? What frustrates you about it?"

*What you're listening for:*
- Pain points with current tool (we can solve)
- Features they value (we need to match)
- Reasons clients abandon tools

*Record feedback:* _______

**Q9: "How satisfied are you with your current case management and client communication systems on a scale of 1-10?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- 1-5 = Strong dissatisfaction, easy sell
- 6-8 = Room for improvement
- 9-10 = Either lying or not a good fit

*Record number:* _______

---

### Section 4: Value Validation (2 minutes)

**Q10: "If I could give you a tool that reduced unnecessary client communication by 70%, what would that be worth to you?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Time value calculation
- Interest level
- Skepticism (address later)

*Record their answer:* _______

**Q11: "What if that same tool automatically compiled all communications, expenses, and compliance data into court-ready exports—cutting your prep time in half?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Eyes lighting up (figuratively)
- "That would be amazing"
- Questions about how it works

*Record their reaction:* _______

**Q12: "If this tool existed and worked as described, what would be a fair monthly price for your firm?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Their price sensitivity
- How they value the solution
- Whether they'd pay anything at all

*Record their pricing feedback:* _______

**Your Script:**
"Thanks for that context—super helpful. So here's what we've built: CommonGround is an AI-powered co-parenting platform designed specifically for high-conflict cases. The core feature is ARIA, our AI that mediates parent communication in real-time—it rewrites hostile messages before they're sent, preventing about 80% of unnecessary conflicts.

For attorneys like you, it means your clients resolve scheduling, expenses, and logistics themselves. Everything creates court-admissible records with SHA-256 verification. When you DO need to go to court, you export perfect documentation in one click.

Our beta attorneys have reduced client calls by 67% and case prep time by 70%.

Would it be worth 30 minutes to see a demo?"

**If yes:** 
"Fantastic. I have [Time Slots] available. Which works better for you?"

**If no:**
"No problem. Can I ask what's holding you back?"
*[Address objection, offer to follow up, or gracefully exit]*

## Discovery Call Scripts - Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: They're Already Using OurFamilyWizard

**Attorney:** "We already use OurFamilyWizard with our clients."

**Your Response:**
"That's great—OFW paved the way for platforms like ours. What do you like about it?"

*[Let them answer]*

"And what frustrates you about it?"

*[Let them answer - likely: expensive, outdated UI, clients don't stick with it]*

"Got it. So CommonGround is essentially OFW rebuilt for 2026. We kept what works—court-admissible records, structured communication—but added AI mediation that prevents conflicts before they happen. And our interface is mobile-first, so clients actually use it. The attorney portal is also much more robust for case management. Would it be worth seeing the difference in a demo?"

### Scenario 2: They're Skeptical of AI

**Attorney:** "I don't know about AI making decisions about custody cases."

**Your Response:**
"That's a really smart concern, and I want to be clear: ARIA doesn't make decisions. Parents do. ARIA suggests—parents decide. 

Here's how it works: a parent writes 'You're such a selfish jerk for forgetting his soccer cleats.' ARIA suggests 'Can we make sure all sports equipment goes in both homes? This way, nothing gets forgotten.' The parent sees both versions side-by-side and chooses.

ARIA was trained by family law experts and child psychologists specifically for co-parenting communication. It's not a general chatbot making things up. And every interaction creates verified records you control.

The AI is a tool for conflict prevention—not a decision-maker. Does that make more sense?"

### Scenario 3: They Say It's Too Expensive for Their Clients

**Attorney:** "My clients can't afford another monthly subscription."

**Your Response:**
"I totally understand that concern. Let me flip the question: how much does one preventable conflict cost them in your billable hours?"

*[They'll say $200-500]*

"Right. So CommonGround Pro is $39.99/month. That's less than one email exchange with you. Our users reduce attorney communication by 73% on average—which pays for the platform many times over.

Also, we have case vouchers where YOUR firm buys access and passes the cost through as a legal expense—clients don't even have to manage the subscription.

Plus, we have financial hardship programs for low-income clients and DV survivor grants.

The question isn't 'can they afford it'—it's 'can they afford NOT to have it?' Because the alternative is hemorrhaging money on preventable legal fees."

### Scenario 4: They Don't Have Time to Learn New Software

**Attorney:** "I'm too busy to learn another platform."

**Your Response:**
"I hear you—and that's exactly why we built this. The demo takes 15 minutes. Onboarding takes 30 minutes. After that? You're saving 15-20 hours per week.

We handle ALL client onboarding and training—not you. You literally just log in when you need to check on a case or export for court.

Our most skeptical attorney told us 'I should have done this on day one of my practice because of how much time it saves.'

Can I show you how simple it is in a 15-minute demo? If you're not convinced it'll save you time, we'll both know in 15 minutes."

### Scenario 5: They're Happy with Current System

**Attorney:** "Honestly, we're doing fine with our current setup."

**Your Response:**
"That's great to hear! Can I ask: what are you currently using?"

*[They describe their system]*

"Got it. And on a scale of 1-10, how satisfied are you with how well your clients follow agreements and communicate appropriately?"

*[They'll rarely say 10]*

"So there's still room for improvement. Look, I'm not here to fix what isn't broken. But if I could show you how to get from [Their Number] to a 9 or 10, would that be worth 15 minutes?"

## Success Metrics to Track

### Quantity Metrics
- Number of discovery calls completed per week
- Number of demos scheduled
- Conversion rate: Calls → Demos
- Conversion rate: Demos → Pilots
- Conversion rate: Pilots → Paid

### Quality Metrics
- Average qualification score
- Time from first contact to demo
- Time from demo to decision
- Referral rate (attorneys referring other attorneys)

### Intelligence Metrics
- Top 3 pain points mentioned
- Most common tools currently used
- Average time spent on case management
- Price sensitivity ranges
- Objections by frequency

## Weekly Review Ritual

Every Friday, review your attorney tracker and ask:

1. **Volume:** Did I hit my target number of calls this week?
2. **Quality:** What was my average qualification score?
3. **Patterns:** What pain points came up repeatedly?
4. **Conversion:** How many demos did I schedule?
5. **Learning:** What new objection did I hear, and how can I handle it better?
6. **Referrals:** How many new attorney names did I collect?
7. **Pipeline:** What's my projected MRR from this week's activity in 90 days?

## Attorney Discovery Call Training

### Role Play Scenarios

**Practice these with a colleague before going live:**

1. **The Skeptic** - Attorney who doesn't believe AI can help
2. **The Busy One** - Attorney who has no time for anything
3. **The Competitor User** - Happy with OurFamilyWizard
4. **The Budget-Conscious** - Worried about cost
5. **The Tech-Challenged** - Doesn't want to learn new tools

**Record yourself doing these role plays and listen back for:**
- Are you asking open-ended questions?
- Are you listening more than talking?
- Are you addressing concerns directly?
- Are you confident without being pushy?
- Are you creating urgency without being aggressive?
