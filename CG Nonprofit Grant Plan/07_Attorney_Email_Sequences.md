# Attorney Email Outreach Sequences

## Overview
This document contains proven email templates for reaching out to family law attorneys in the SoCal market. Each sequence is designed to be personalized, concise, and focused on the attorney's pain points.

---

## Email Sequence 1: Cold Outreach (Primary)

### Email 1A: The Time-Saver Angle

**Subject Line Options:**
1. "15 hours back per week?" 
2. "Quick question about your high-conflict cases"
3. "How much time do you spend on client texts?"

```

Quick question: What percentage of your time goes to texts, calls, and emails about schedule changes versus actually preparing for court?

Most attorneys we work with were spending 8+ hours weekly on preventable client conflicts—until they started using AI-mediated communication tools.

Would it be worth a 15-minute call to see how this works?

Best,
[Your Name]
[Title]
CommonGround
[Phone]
[Calendar Link]

P.S. - No sales pitch. Just showing you what's working for family law attorneys in LA County.
```

**Why This Works:**
- Opens with specific benefit (time savings)
- Asks engaging question (makes them think)
- Provides social proof (other attorneys)
- Low-commitment CTA (15 minutes)
- P.S. reduces pressure

## Follow-Up Sequence

### Follow-Up 1 (5 Days After Initial Email - If No Response)

**Subject:** "Re: [Original Subject]"

**Email Body:**

```
Hi [Attorney First Name],

Following up on my email from last week about reducing client communication time.

I know you're swamped, so I'll keep this brief: I spoke with [Another Attorney Name] at [Firm Name] in [Nearby City], and they said "I wish I'd found this 5 years ago."

Not saying we're right for every practice, but if you're dealing with high-conflict cases that monopolize your time, it's worth a quick look.

15 minutes on your calendar? [Calendar Link]

If not, no worries—just let me know and I'll stop bothering you.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Why This Works:**
- Acknowledges they're busy
- Provides social proof (named attorney if possible)
- Gives them an out (permission to say no)
- Maintains casual, non-pushy tone

---

**Email Body:**

```

Here's a 3-minute video demo: [Link to Demo Video]

```

**Why This Works:**
- "Last email" creates urgency without pressure
- Provides video demo (low commitment)
- Respectful exit (won't bug you again)
- Shows you respect their time

---

### Follow-Up 3 (30 Days Later - "Break-Up" Email)

**Subject:** "Closing your file"

**Email Body:**

```
Hi [Attorney First Name],

I'm closing my outreach file for [City] attorneys and noticed we never connected.

Before I do, quick question: what would have made my initial email more relevant to your practice?

(I'm genuinely trying to get better at this, so any feedback helps—even if it's "nothing, I'm just not interested.")

Thanks,
[Your Name]

P.S. - If you know another family law attorney in your network who deals with high-conflict cases, I'd appreciate an intro.
```

**Why This Works:**
- Unexpected approach (break-up email)
- Asks for feedback (engages them)
- Humble tone (genuinely learning)
- P.S. asks for referrals
- Often gets responses when nothing else worked

### Tracking Metrics

For each email, track:
- **Sent:** Number of emails sent
- **Open Rate:** % who opened
- **Reply Rate:** % who replied
- **Positive Reply Rate:** % who replied with interest
- **Meeting Booked:** % who scheduled discovery call
- **Meeting Held:** % who actually showed up
- **Demo Scheduled:** % who moved to demo stage
- **Conversion to Pilot:** % who signed up

**Target Benchmarks:**
- Open Rate: 30-40% (cold outreach)
- Reply Rate: 5-10%
- Positive Reply Rate: 2-5%
- Meeting Booked: 1-3%

## Email Deliverability Best Practices

To avoid spam filters:

1. **Warm Up Your Domain:**
   - Start with 10-20 emails/day
   - Gradually increase volume
   - Get replies to improve sender reputation

2. **Authentication:**
   - Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records
   - Use professional email domain (not Gmail/Yahoo for outreach)

3. **Avoid Spam Triggers:**
   - Don't use ALL CAPS in subject lines
   - Avoid excessive punctuation (!!!)
   - Don't use spam words (free, guaranteed, act now)
   - Include physical address in signature
   - Easy unsubscribe option

4. **Keep Clean Lists:**
   - Remove hard bounces immediately
   - Don't email people who've unsubscribed
   - Verify email addresses before sending

5. **Monitor Metrics:**
   - If open rates drop below 15%, you might be landing in spam
   - High bounce rates hurt sender reputation
   - Track spam complaints
