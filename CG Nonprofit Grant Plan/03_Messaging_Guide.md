## Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Attributes

**1. Empathetic but Not Pitying**
- ✅ "We know co-parenting after divorce is incredibly difficult"
- ❌ "We feel so sorry for your terrible situation"

**2. Authoritative but Not Condescending**
- ✅ "Research shows that AI-mediated communication reduces conflict by 68%"
- ❌ "Obviously, you need our help because you can't communicate properly"

**3. Hopeful but Not Pollyannaish**
- ✅ "Peaceful co-parenting is possible, even in high-conflict situations"
- ❌ "You'll be best friends with your ex in no time!"

**4. Professional but Not Corporate**
- ✅ "We're building technology that protects children"
- ❌ "We leverage cutting-edge AI/ML paradigms to optimize stakeholder engagement"

**5. Neutral but Not Cold**
- ✅ "We don't take sides. We help both parents communicate better."
- ❌ "We have no feelings about your situation whatsoever"

## Competitor Positioning

**Them:** Established, court-recognized, but outdated interface and no AI
**Us:** Modern, AI-powered, same legal credibility, better experience, lower cost

**Positioning:**
"OurFamilyWizard was revolutionary in 2001. CommonGround is revolutionary for 2026."

**Key Differentiators:**
- ARIA AI mediation (they have zero AI)
- Modern, mobile-first interface
- Lower cost ($39.99 vs. ~$32/family but way more features)
- Better attorney tools

**When to mention:**
- When prospects say "I've heard of OFW"
- In attorney sales conversations
- In court presentations

**How to position:**
"OurFamilyWizard paved the way for platforms like ours—we owe them a debt. But they built for 2001. We built for 2026. The core is the same—court-admissible records, structured communication—but we added AI mediation, modern UX, and attorney tools that actually work."

**Them:** Cheap, simple, but very limited features
**Us:** Full-featured, same price point, massively more capability

**Positioning:**
"Talking Parents is like a notepad. CommonGround is a legal operating system."

**Key Differentiators:**
- AI mediation vs. basic messaging
- Agreement builder (they have none)
- Professional portal (they have bare minimum)
- Financial tools (theirs are basic)

**When to mention:**
- When cost is the primary objection
- When prospects say "I just need simple communication"

**How to position:**
"Talking Parents works great if you have low conflict and just need a message log. But if you're dealing with high conflict, constant disputes, court involvement—you need intelligence, not just a digital notepad. That's where ARIA changes everything."

**Key Differentiators:**

**When to mention:**
- When prospects are skeptical of "another app"

Or you keep using texts, and in 2 years you're in court with a lawyer holding up screenshots that prove nothing. Your choice."

**Act 1: The Struggle**

## Word Choice Guidelines

### Words to USE

**Empowering:**
- Peaceful, calm, organized, protected, supported, empowered, clear, confident

**Professional:**
- Verified, court-ready, documented, compliant, admissible, evidence, records

**Transformative:**
- Transform, reduce, improve, solve, resolve, mediate, facilitate, enable

**Child-Centric:**
- Protect, shield, support, wellbeing, healthy, thriving, peaceful

**Tech-Forward (but not jargony):**
- AI-powered, intelligent, automated, real-time, secure, verified

### Words to AVOID

**Judgmental:**
- ❌ Toxic, dysfunctional, broken, failed, terrible, awful
- ✅ High-conflict, challenging, difficult

**Overpromising:**
- ❌ Perfect, flawless, guaranteed, never, always, completely
- ✅ Significantly, measurably, typically, often

**Pity-Based:**
- ❌ Poor, suffering, victim, helpless, desperate
- ✅ Survivor, parent, client, user

**Manipulative:**
- ❌ You need this, You can't do this alone, You're making mistakes
- ✅ This helps, Many parents find, Research shows

**Legal Jargon (unless talking to attorneys):**
- ❌ Pursuant to, hereinafter, stipulations
- ✅ According to, from now on, agreements

### Phrases to USE

**Opening Lines:**

## Crisis Communication Guidelines

### If There's a Data Breach

**Message Priority:**
1. Acknowledge immediately and transparently
2. Explain what happened (in plain English)
3. Detail what data was/wasn't affected
4. Outline steps being taken
5. Provide resources for affected users
6. Commit to prevention measures

**Sample Statement:**
"On [Date], we discovered unauthorized access to [System]. We immediately secured the vulnerability and began investigating. Here's what you need to know: [Clear bullets]. If you're affected, here's what we're doing: [Actions]. We take your trust seriously and are implementing [Prevention]. Questions? Contact [Person] directly at [Email]."

### If ARIA Makes a Mistake

**Message Priority:**
1. Acknowledge the error
2. Explain what went wrong
3. Detail the fix
4. Commit to improvement
5. Offer remedy to affected users

**Sample Statement:**
"Yesterday, ARIA incorrectly flagged [Issue] in approximately [Number] messages. This was due to [Technical Reason, Simply Explained]. We've corrected the algorithm and reviewed all affected messages. If your message was impacted, we've [Remedy]. We're sorry for any confusion this caused."

### If a User Experiences Harm

**Message Priority:**
1. Express genuine concern
2. Gather full context
3. Involve appropriate stakeholders (legal, support, safety)
4. Take immediate protective action
5. Follow up personally

**Sample Response:**
"We're deeply concerned about your safety. Your situation requires immediate attention. I'm connecting you with [Crisis Resource] right now. In parallel, we're [Immediate Action on Platform]. You can reach me directly at [Contact] 24/7. Your safety is our absolute priority."

## Messaging Dos and Don'ts Summary

### DO:
✅ Lead with empathy and child wellbeing
✅ Use data and proof points to build credibility
✅ Speak plainly—avoid jargon unless audience-appropriate
✅ Acknowledge the difficulty of co-parenting
✅ Celebrate small wins and progress
✅ Maintain absolute neutrality between co-parents
✅ Focus on outcomes, not features
✅ Provide clear next steps and CTAs
✅ Use testimonials and real stories
✅ Be transparent about limitations

### DON'T:
❌ Make divorce or conflict feel trivial
❌ Overpromise on what AI can do
❌ Take sides between co-parents
❌ Use fear-based marketing
❌ Speak negatively about competitors
❌ Use complex legal or technical language unnecessarily
❌ Assume all families look the same
❌ Ignore cultural sensitivity
❌ Rush people to buy
❌ Pretend we're perfect

## Message Testing Checklist

Before publishing ANY message, ask:

**✓ Empathy Check:**
Does this message show understanding of the user's situation?

**✓ Clarity Check:**
Could a stressed, exhausted parent understand this in 5 seconds?

**✓ Neutrality Check:**
Does this message avoid taking sides between co-parents?

**✓ Truth Check:**
Can we back up every claim with data or testimonials?

**✓ Child-First Check:**
Does this message prioritize child wellbeing?

**✓ Action Check:**
Is it clear what the user should do next?

**✓ Brand Voice Check:**
Does this sound like CommonGround (empathetic, professional, hopeful)?
