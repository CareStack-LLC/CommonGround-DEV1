# Nonprofit Partnership Discovery Call Script & Tracker

## Purpose
This script helps you identify, qualify, and onboard nonprofit partners who serve DV survivors and high-conflict families. Use this for ALL nonprofit partnership conversations.

---

## Pre-Call Preparation (10 minutes)

### Research the Organization
- [ ] Website reviewed (mission, programs, size)
- [ ] Annual report reviewed (if available)
- [ ] Social media presence checked
- [ ] Key staff identified (Executive Director, Program Director)
- [ ] Funding sources understood (grants, donations, government)
- [ ] Families served per year estimated
- [ ] Service area and locations noted

### Key Information to Gather
- Organization name and location
- Primary contact (name, title, email)
- Annual families served
- Types of services provided
- Current tools/resources offered to clients
- Grant reporting requirements

---

## Call Structure (30 minutes total)

### Opening (2 minutes)

**Your Script:**
"Hi [Contact Name], this is [Your Name] from CommonGround. Thanks so much for making time to talk. I know how busy you are serving your community.

Quick context: CommonGround is building technology to support families navigating high-conflict co-parenting, and we're specifically focused on partnering with organizations like [Nonprofit Name] that serve DV survivors and families in crisis.

I'd love to learn about your programs and explore if there's a way we can support your mission. Does that sound good? Perfect—can I start by asking you a few questions about [Nonprofit Name]?"

## Discovery Questions (20 minutes)

### Section 1: Organization Overview (5 minutes)

**Q1: "Tell me about [Nonprofit Name]. What's your core mission and who do you serve?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- DV survivors (primary target)
- Family crisis services (secondary target)
- Legal aid (great fit)
- General family services (possible fit)
- Single parents (possible fit)

*Record their mission:* _______

**Q2: "Approximately how many families do you serve per year?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- 100+ families = Great scale for impact
- 50-100 families = Good starting point
- <50 families = May be too small for initial pilot

*Record number:* _______

**Q3: "What percentage of the families you work with are dealing with custody or co-parenting challenges?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- 50%+ = Perfect fit
- 25-50% = Good fit
- <25% = May not be the right partner

*Record percentage:* _______

**Q4: "Walk me through a typical client journey. How long do they stay in your program?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Crisis intervention (short-term, 2-8 weeks)
- Long-term support (months to years)
- Transitional programs (3-6 months)
- Drop-in services (ongoing but inconsistent)

*Record program structure:* _______

**Q5: "What happens after clients complete your program? Do you provide ongoing support?"**

*What you're listening for:*
- Pain point: "We lose touch with them"
- Pain point: "We don't have capacity for follow-up"
- Pain point: "We wonder if they're okay but have no way to check"
- Opportunity: "We refer them to other services"

## Transition to Proposal (1 minute)

**Your Script:**
"Thank you for all that context—super helpful. Based on what you've shared, I think there's a really strong fit here. Let me tell you what we're proposing:

CommonGround is an AI-powered co-parenting platform that helps your clients communicate safely with their co-parent after they leave your program. Our AI, ARIA, mediates every message—removing hostility and creating court-ready documentation.

Here's what we'd provide to [Nonprofit Name]:

1. **Free access for all your clients** - Pro tier ($40/month value), 180 days minimum, no cost to you or them
2. **Co-branded landing page** - [YourNonprofit].CommonGround.app showing YOUR organization's support
3. **Automated outcome tracking** - Dashboard showing client usage, conflict reduction, engagement
4. **Grant-ready reports** - One-click exports in any format your funders need
5. **Marketing support** - We'll write blog posts and case studies highlighting your program
6. **Priority support** - Direct line to our team for your staff and clients
7. **Training** - 1-hour webinar for your staff, resources for clients

Your part: Distribute grant codes to qualified clients and provide a 15-minute orientation using materials we give you.

What do you think? Does this sound like something [Nonprofit Name] would benefit from?"

**Wait for response and address questions**

## Addressing Common Questions (5 minutes)

### "How do we know this is safe for our clients?"

**Your Response:**
"That's the most important question, and I'm glad you asked. Safety is literally why we built this.

ARIA is trauma-informed and was designed with DV advocates. It monitors every message for red flags—threats, manipulation, escalation. If we detect danger signals, we alert you immediately and can escalate to emergency services if needed.

Your clients control their data. Everything is encrypted. Co-parents can't see each other's contact info. We have restraining order modes that block direct contact.

And most importantly: ARIA prevents hostile messages from ever reaching your clients. It's a shield, not just documentation.

We're happy to have your legal or safety team review our security and privacy policies before you decide."

### "Our clients don't have smartphones or tech skills"

**Your Response:**
"We hear this concern a lot, and the data actually surprises people. 94% of Americans ages 18-49 have smartphones, including low-income populations. And CommonGround is simpler than texting—ARIA does the hard work.

For the 6% without smartphones, we have web access on any computer or tablet. We also partner with Lifeline and free phone programs.

But here's the thing: if your clients can use Instagram or WhatsApp, they can use CommonGround. We've had 70-year-old grandmothers use it successfully.

Plus, we handle all onboarding and training. Your staff doesn't need to become tech support."

### "What if they can't afford it after 180 days?"

**Your Response:**
"Great question. Three things:

First, we have a financial hardship program—sliding scale down to $9.99/month based on income.

Second, most clients save $8,400/year in legal fees by using CommonGround, so it pays for itself many times over.

Third, our goal is to extend access beyond 180 days through additional grant funding as we grow. We want this to be long-term support, not a short-term Band-Aid.

But honestly, we've found that once clients experience the reduction in conflict, they prioritize keeping the subscription because the alternative—going back to chaotic communication—is too costly emotionally and financially."

### "How do we measure if this is actually working?"

**Your Response:**
"You'll have real-time access to a dashboard showing:
- How many clients activated their accounts
- Usage frequency (logins, messages sent)
- ARIA intervention trends (conflict reduction over time)
- Compliance metrics (on-time exchanges, financial transparency)
- Client satisfaction scores (we survey at 30, 90, 180 days)

You can log in anytime and see exactly how your clients are doing. We'll also send quarterly reports automatically.

And we'll work with you to define custom success metrics that match your grant requirements. If your funder wants specific data points, we'll track them."

### "What's the catch? Why is this free?"

**Your Response:**
"Fair question! Here's our model:

We're building CommonGround as a for-profit social enterprise. We make money from families who can afford to pay and from attorneys who use our professional tools.

But we know DV survivors and low-income families can't afford $40/month, and they're the ones who need it most. So we're subsidizing their access through our CommonGround Cares program.

Why? Three reasons:

1. **Mission alignment** - Our founder [Your Story] started this because of [Personal Connection to Problem].
2. **Market validation** - DV survivors and high-conflict families prove the platform works in the toughest situations.
3. **Brand building** - When you tell your network about us, when clients succeed and share their stories, that's worth more than $40/month.

So yes, there's a business case. But it's not extractive—it's symbiotic. We grow by genuinely helping people, not by exploiting them."

## Nonprofit Partnership Pilot Program

### Phase 1: Onboarding (Weeks 1-2)

**Week 1:**
- Partnership agreement signed
- Co-branded landing page created
- Grant code system set up (25 codes initially)
- Staff training scheduled

**Week 2:**
- Staff training completed (1 hour webinar)
- Client orientation materials delivered
- First grant codes distributed
- Tracking dashboard access provided

### Phase 2: Launch & Support (Weeks 3-8)

**Week 3-4:**
- First clients activate accounts
- Monitor activation rate (target: 60%+)
- Daily check-ins with nonprofit point of contact
- Address any technical or onboarding issues

**Week 5-8:**
- Weekly data review with nonprofit
- Collect client feedback via surveys
- Identify successful patterns and barriers
- Adjust onboarding process as needed

### Phase 3: Optimization (Weeks 9-12)

**Week 9:**
- 30-day client survey results analyzed
- First outcomes report generated
- Success stories identified for case studies

**Week 10:**
- Mid-pilot review meeting with nonprofit leadership
- Discuss expansion (more codes, more programs)
- Begin grant reporting language development

**Week 11-12:**
- 90-day outcomes report
- Case study development (if nonprofit agrees)
- Plan for long-term partnership
- Discuss referrals to other nonprofits in network

### Success Criteria (90 Days)

**Activation Metrics:**
- 60%+ of issued codes activated
- 50%+ of activated users still active at 90 days

**Outcome Metrics:**
- 25%+ measurable conflict reduction
- 80%+ clients report feeling safer
- 70%+ clients report reduced stress

**Satisfaction Metrics:**
- Client NPS > 50
- Nonprofit staff satisfaction > 4/5
- Would refer us to other nonprofits: Yes

**Operational Metrics:**
- Grant reporting time reduced by 50%+
- Nonprofit can serve 20%+ more families with same staff
- Zero serious safety incidents

## Objection Handling Cheat Sheet

| Objection | Quick Response |
|-----------|----------------|
| "Clients can't afford it" | "That's exactly why it's free for your clients—no cost to them or you" |

## Key Differentiators to Emphasize

### vs. Traditional Referrals
- **Them:** Give clients a list of attorneys/resources, hope they follow through
- **Us:** Provide ongoing 24/7 support that extends your program's impact

### vs. One-Time Workshops
- **Them:** Client learns co-parenting strategies in 2-hour workshop
- **Us:** AI coach guides them through every real interaction for months

### vs. Case Management Software
- **Them:** Tools for YOU to track clients
- **Us:** Tools for CLIENTS to manage their lives + you get outcome data

### vs. Static Resource Lists
- **Them:** Handout with phone numbers
- **Us:** Active technology platform with intelligence and documentation

## Success Story Collection Framework

### At 30 Days (First Check-In)

**Questions to Ask Nonprofit:**
1. Which clients are most engaged with the platform?
2. Any success stories we should know about?
3. What's working well? What's challenging?
4. Any requests for features or improvements?

**Questions for Clients (Anonymous Survey):**
1. How has CommonGround affected your co-parenting communication? (1-5 scale)
2. Do you feel safer communicating with your co-parent? (Yes/No/Somewhat)
3. What's been most helpful about the platform?
4. What would you improve?
5. Would you recommend this to other parents in your situation? (Yes/No)

### At 90 Days (Case Study Development)

**Ideal Case Study Candidate:**
- High conflict at start → measurable improvement
- Willing to share story (anonymously or with first name only)
- Clear before/after narrative
- Represents nonprofit's typical client

**Case Study Structure:**
1. **Background:** "Maria's situation when she came to [Nonprofit Name]"
2. **Challenge:** "Co-parent was hostile, afraid for safety, chaos with scheduling"
3. **Solution:** "How [Nonprofit Name] provided CommonGround access"
4. **Results:** "90 days later: X% less conflict, saved $Y in legal fees, daughter's anxiety reduced"
5. **Quote:** "CommonGround gave me peace of mind..." - Maria, [City]
6. **Nonprofit Impact:** "Maria is one of 50 clients [Nonprofit Name] supports through CommonGround"

## Weekly Review Ritual

Every Friday, review your nonprofit tracker and ask:

1. **Volume:** How many new nonprofits did I contact this week?
2. **Pipeline:** How many are in active discussions vs. considering?
3. **Launches:** Did any new partnerships go live?
4. **Activation:** What's the overall activation rate across all partners?
5. **Satisfaction:** Any red flags from current partners?
6. **Learning:** What objection came up repeatedly and how can I address it better?
7. **Referrals:** How many new organizations did current partners introduce us to?
